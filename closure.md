# Closure

Persistence shows that some structures do not fail. The next question is how to understand such persistence more precisely.

We will refer to structures that persist as closures.

A closure is a structure whose conditions do not lead to its failure and which is not eliminated under the conditions in which it appears. It maintains itself without requiring external enforcement or foundational prescription.

Closure does not imply permanence. It does not require duration in a fundamental sense, nor does it assume a background time through which it endures. It simply refers to the persistence of a structure under the conditions present.

Closures may arise directly through generativity or through relations among existing structures. The framework does not assume a single mode of appearance.

Not all structures are closures. Many structures may fail. Closures are those that persist.

Later notes will examine how closures may vary in stability and how they may give rise to more complex structural regimes.
