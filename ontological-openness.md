# Ontological Openness

Non-governance removes foundational prescription. Reality is not assumed to rest on axioms, rules, or prior principles fixing what must exist or how things must behave.

Once this assumption is set aside, what remains is ontological openness. Nothing at the base mandates structure, but nothing prohibits it either. No prior rule determines what must appear, and no foundational constraints fix what cannot.

Under non-governance, structure is not imposed in advance. Coherence does not originate from prescription. It arises, if it arises at all, without foundational mandate.

Stability therefore does not require enforcement. A structure need not be dictated by prior rules in order to endure; it need only avoid eliminating itself.

In this framework the central question changes. Instead of asking what rules produce or maintain reality, we ask what forms of coherence, if any, persist without foundational prescription.

Later notes will examine how such persistence can arise.
