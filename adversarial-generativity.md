# Adversarial Generativity

Generativity allows structure to arise when no foundational rules prohibit its appearance. But generativity does not imply that structures appear alone.

Under non-governance, nothing coordinates how many structures may arise or how they may relate. Multiple structures may therefore appear without regard for one another.

When structures arise together, they need not be compatible. Their conditions may overlap, contradict, or undermine one another. Some combinations may reinforce stability, while others may interfere.

Structures may also fail independently of such interference. Generativity permits structures whose own conditions do not sustain them.

Generativity is therefore not merely productive. It is also adversarial. Structures may encounter other structures whose conditions conflict with their own.

This interference follows simply from the absence of foundational coordination among structures.

As a result, many structures may fail to persist. Later notes will examine how persistence can nevertheless arise under such conditions.
