# Stability and Reinforcement

Closures persist, but persistence need not be uniform. Some closures may remain only under limited conditions, while others may persist more broadly.

Reinforcement refers to relations among closures that contribute to their persistence. Closures that reinforce one another may remain under conditions in which they might otherwise fail.

Reinforcement does not arise from foundational coordination. It follows from the structures of closures and how they relate. Compatible relations may therefore allow some closures to persist in ways that would not be available to them in isolation.

Stability refers to persistence that is maintained across a wider range of conditions.

Not all closures are stable. Some may persist only under narrow conditions, while others may persist more broadly depending on their relations.

Later notes will examine how compatible, coexisting closures may give rise to more complex structures and how such structures may form consistent structural regimes.
