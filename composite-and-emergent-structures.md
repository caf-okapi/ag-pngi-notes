# Composite and Emergent Structures

Closures may relate, reinforcing, neutral to, or undermining one another. Such relations may give rise to further structure.

When structures relate, the relations themselves may form structure. Structure need not be reducible to any single structure in isolation, nor limited to relations among multiple structures. Some structure may arise from relations internal to a structure, while other structure may arise through relations among multiple structures.

Structures that arise in these ways may be described as composite or emergent. This does not imply a hierarchy or a fundamental level. It simply reflects that some structures are associated with relations, whether internal or among multiple structures.

These structures may also persist. When they do, they are closures.

The framework does not assume a single mode of appearance. Structures may arise directly through generativity or through relations among existing structures.

Later notes will examine how such structures may form consistent regimes and how these regimes may be associated with what are commonly described as space, time, or other domains.
