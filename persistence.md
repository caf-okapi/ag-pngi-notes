# Persistence

Generativity permits the appearance of structure. Structures may fail, whether through interaction with other structures or through their own conditions.

But generativity does not privilege either failure or persistence. Just as there is no foundational rule requiring structures to endure, there is no foundational rule requiring them to fail.

Neither persistence nor failure is prohibited.

A structure may persist when its conditions do not lead to its failure and it is not eliminated under the conditions in which it appears. This requires no external enforcement and no prior coordination. It follows from the absence of foundational restriction.

Under non-governance, both persistence and non-persistence are possible.

Later notes will examine how such persistence may be maintained and how persistent structures may give rise to further structural organization.
