# Closure as the Basis of Reality

Generativity permits structure to appear. Structures may fail and may encounter adversarial conditions. Some structures persist. These persistent structures are closures.

Closure is therefore not an exceptional outcome. It is a natural consequence of generativity and the absence of foundational restriction.

Under non-governance, there is no separate underlying layer that gives rise to reality. There are no foundational rules, no prior space, and no external substrate organizing what exists.

What persists are closures.

Reality, in this framework, consists of closures.

Closures do not exist within a pre-existing world; they constitute what is present.

Later notes will examine how closures may differ in stability and how more complex forms of structure may arise from relations among closures.
