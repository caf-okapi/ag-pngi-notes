# Degrees and Types of Closure

Closures are structures that persist. But persistence does not imply uniformity. Closures need not all be alike.

Some closures may persist only under limited conditions. Others may persist more broadly. Some may be easily disrupted, while others may remain despite a wider range of conditions.

Closures may also differ in how they relate to other structures. Some may remain compatible with many structures, while others may fail under even slight variation.

These differences do not arise from foundational rules. They follow from the structures themselves and the conditions in which they appear.

Closures therefore admit degrees. Persistence is not a single, uniform property, but one that may vary across structures.

Later notes will examine how such differences may give rise to more complex patterns of structure and relation.
